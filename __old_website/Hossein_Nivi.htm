Dr. Hossein Nivi

January 27, 2005

 

My dear friend, my dear younger brother, Dr. Farhang Aslani passed away recently. Knowing Farhang has been a privilege for me in many dimensions. He has been a role model, mentor, guide, financial counselor and many more. Farhang was a man of great strength facing the biggest adversity any one can face; he faced the life or death situation with greater courage that I have never seen.

 

Nasrin called me on a cold Sunday evening and informed me of Farhang's final decision to stop all treatments. She told that his situation is terminal. I immediately went to the hospital. I spent from 9:30 in the evening to well past midnight with Farhang and Nasrin.

Farhang was talking very positively. He was sharp, witty and full of life. He was advising both of us on what to do and how life goes on.

 

Nasrin said, "all of that is right, everyone will terminate at one point or the other, but this is too soon. We love you and we need you to be around. This is too soon," while tears ran down her face. Farhang, emphatically and with a very strong voice said, "you should not mourn my death, but celebrate my life. I want you to look forward and gain strength from this event. I want to be remembered positively."

 

So let's celebrate his life and always remember him the way he wanted. He will be our guide, our mentor, our role model, and the light showing the way.

 

A wise man summed up the purpose of life as: To Live, To Love, To Learn, And To Leave a Legacy

 

To Live: Farhang lived a very full life, his wife Nasrin, two daughters Bahareh and Lauleh and his son Mohammad Reza.

He lived every moment of his life positively; I remember I was on a trip with Farhang to Iran meeting with a group of industrialist and academicians. He was just coming off of another session of Chemotherapy few days earlier. He was physically week and fragile. But you should have seen him when he got up to talk; he was full of life energetic, articulate, smart and witty. I could not believe my eyes. 

 

And To Love: Farhang loved his family very dearly…. He loved his friends…… He loved Ford Motor Company….. He loved his job…. He loved the universities he graduated from…..  He loved everything in life unconditionally.

 

And To Learn: Farhang was a learner, SUT, UofM, MBA, MS, PhD. He has five postgraduate degrees. I suppose he was the only one who has more degrees than a thermometer. 

Constantly reading and learning…. I was amazed at his appetite for learning…. He learned the complex process of designing cars, and then he learned finance and managed a large portfolio for Ford Motor Company….. He could move from science to engineering, to poetry, back to finance, and then talk about philosophy and humanity.

 

And to Leave a Legacy: Farhang left a great legacy for all of us. The most important legacy is his family. He touched many lives across the globe. He was a leader in the Middle Eastern Community at Ford. The carpet that he designed is hanging in Ford Motor Company's WHQ Lobby. He left a legacy at Ford in product development, finance, treasury, add many others. He left a legacy in his community. He left a legacy in Iran, his home country and the USA, his adopted country. He touched our lives in ways that we will never forget.

 

So let's celebrate the life of Dr. Farhang Aslani. He never left us, he will never leave us, and he will always remain in our hearts. Thank you.
