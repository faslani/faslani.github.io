Dr. Kamran Aslani

January 27, 2005

 

On behalf of Farhang's family and myself, I wanted to thank every one for coming to this ceremony today. Especially, I would like to thank those who were visiting Farhang during his fight with cancer either in the hospital or at his home during the past 3 years. Some of you were coming from so far away for such a visit.  I know this was not easy for you!

 

I also like to thank Mr. Visintainer  and Mr. Nivi for their kind words about Farhang. I have a special thank for Dr. Sara Hashemian, Dr. Ozbecki, Dr. Sarabandi, and Mr. Agah, Mr. Mossalaei and so many others that I apologize that I can not name them all due to time constrain. Last but not the least, I also wanted to thank Nasrin for taking care of Farhang every minute of the last 3 years.

 

Farhang was a special man. He made himself an example for personal and professional honesty and integrity who had a great respect for others and strong sense of forgiveness. I remember him telling me in many occasions "Treat others the way you like to be treated".  I have heard from so many people that "he was my mentor". These were the people whose life was positively affected by coming in contact with him. I, for one, was one of them.

 

If you did a very small thing for him, despite his busy life, he would definitely call to thank you for that.

 

He was available to anybody who needed his advice despite his rank in Ford Motor Company. I remember I had a Chinese classmate that was trying to decide what to do after he graduated from MBA program. Knowing that Farhang was my brother, He asked me if he could talk to Farhang. I asked him why don't you send him an email. Although Farhang did not know him, sure enough, he got an appointment in response to his email from Farhang and was able to consult with him about his future. I can easily count close to 20 people who decided to go to MBA program after engineering degree by coming in contact with him. I can also count quite a few that followed his example to go to the Financial Engineering program.

 

He also had a great sense of forgiveness. Let me tell you a story about him when we were living in our parents' house and none of us were married. I remember I was 14 years old. The year was 1976.  Farhang just bought a brand new 1976 Fiat. I just loved driving his car in the afternoon when he came home. He was a sophomore then. I remember one afternoon in November I smashed his brand new car that he purchased just 6 months before. After the accident the car looked more like a bow tie as it was hit in the passenger side in B pillar location. I do not remember him telling me a single word about what happened, why it happened, or how it happened. As a matter of fact, I do not even recall having a conversation about the subject. Instead, he was home after the incident making sure I do not feel bad. Maybe he wanted to compensate for the time he would take my bike and run a way when I was 10 years old!

 

He was always there when I needed him. I will miss him dearly especially now that I know I will not hear his voice on the other side of my # 3 speed dial of my cell phone

 

At the end, I would like to say that I do not feel that Farhang is gone. His great characters are still here in his 3 wonderful children, Bahareh, Lauleh, and Mohammad-Reza and I am proud to be their uncle.

 

I will leave you with a translation of a poem that he likes to be on his grave:

 

I am a bird from Heaven; I am not from this earth.

I'm confined to this world by my body for not very long.

I am looking forward to the moment that I go back to the Lord.

I will be so happy like a bird flying for a joyful destination.
