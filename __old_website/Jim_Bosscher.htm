Jim Bosscher

January 21, 2005

 

Good Morning, my name is Jim Bosscher.

 

I appreciate the opportunity to say a few words about my very good friend, Farhang Aslani.

 

I have known Farhang for only about 10 years—but it seems like I have known him all my life.

 

I first met Farhang when we worked together in Treasury at Ford Motor Company.

 

Wall street had hired “rocket scientists” to develop complex financing structures to market to its clients and we recognized the need to have one of our own—Farhang was that man.  He had already enjoyed an outstanding career in the Engineering Dept at Ford, and with his various advanced engineering and business degrees, he was a natural for our position.  When his outstanding professional and academic background were combined with his outstanding work ethic, leadership skills, and drive for results he and his team were able to make an incredible impact on Ford Credit’s financing strategy.  Those financing initiatives launched by Farhang and his team became the backbone for the financing strategies Ford Credit continues to rely on today. 

 

Farhang was a passionate man and he loved Ford Motor Company.  He played many other important roles at Ford:

 

Recruiting—he just loved to going back to his alma mater, U-M, and recruiting students.  The students responded positively to his unquenchable thirst for knowledge and his passion for Ford Motor co.  He felt very passionate about the University of Michigan and did what he could to support it.  His strong feelings about both Ford and the University benefited both entities.

 

Farhang was not only a great student but also a great teacher and mentor--he was one of the “go-to” guys for knowledge.  People of all levels would seek him out for advice and counsel about a variety of topics.

 

He was a great mentor to Ford employees and he also participated as a mentor with me in a program with a Detroit Elementary School.  His son Mohammad often participated with him and together with the rest of the family they touched the lives of several students and then took the idea to the Arab American community where he established a similar program.  Working with young people came very naturally to Farhang.

 

In addition to feeding his mind, Farhang was also conscious of the need to stay in good physical shape so he took up Judo.  I can’t remember if he got his black belt or not be was certainly moving in that direction.  He even convinced me to try out the sport—the guy was so persuasive.

 

I was also able to see what a wonderful family man he was.  If you want to really get an appreciation for a wonderful person Farhang was—spend time with his children.  Farhang and Nasrin have instilled all their wonderful characteristics in them.  They are intelligent, respectful, self confident, mindful of others, and friendly.  They are so very special.

 

Farhang was an incredible optimist—the glass was always half full—the word empty was never mentioned.

         

During the last three years, Farhang fought the battle with more integrity than one could believe possible.  He and Nasrin applied their skills to research every possible cure but in the end, we all lost as cancer took his life. 

 

This awful disease, however, never touched his spirit—in fact it provided a forum for Farhang to demonstrate his incredible interpersonal strengths.

When I last visited with Farhang a few days ago, I witnessed again these strengths.

         

          He had the ability to make all around him feel special

          To think only the best of others

          To work only for the best

          He was too big to worry,

          Too noble for anger,

          Too strong for fear

          Too happy to permit trouble into his life and

          He was so strong that he always maintained his peace of mind

 

He prepared himself and all around him for what he had come to gracefully accept.  He was as much a model for us in life as he was in the way he accepted death.

 

And Farhang’s spirit lives on through his friends, work colleagues, family—and especially in his incredible children.  And this spirit will be passed on in generations to come:

 

Let me leave you with this one last thought.  As you think about Farhang—today and in the future: Please conjure up an image of his incredibly warm smile and that twinkle in his eyes—he would like that.

 

Thank you.
