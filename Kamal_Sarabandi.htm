Dr. Kamal Sarabandi

January 21, 2005

 

We have gathered to express our respect and love for our very dear friend Farhang. We are here to bid farewell to our beloved friend who has left a positive impression upon our individual lives. I consider myself very fortunate for enjoying his friendship for more than 32 years without interruption. We went to high school together, after graduation attended the same college, fought the same war, came to US to continue our higher education and graduated from University of Michigan almost simultaneously, and stayed in the same city to pay our dues back to the society. I should add here that Farhang’s love for science and education did not stop at the Ph.D level, he continued to expand his knowledge beyond a given discipline and literary up until he was diagnosed with cancer her remained a dedicated scholar.

 

Dear friends, although Farhang’s life was cut short, he lived a colorful life. Farhang was an accomplished person in all aspects of life many of us struggle to fulfill just a fraction of it. He was dearly loved by his family and whoever came to know him, he was respected by his colleagues and peers, and significantly contributed to the local Iranian and Muslim community selflessly. Farhang was an icon and showed many of us how to conduct our lives and deal with insurmountable challenges we may face. I vividly remember the way he always wanted to disguise the difficulties and paint you a rosy picture of the tasks at hand. He always wanted you to see the flowers for the flower and not for the thorns.   I still remember his confident voice during the college years when going through difficult exams, a kind of individual who sought worthy challenges to extract the joy of life by coming through triumphant.

 

We miss him enormously and his departure has created such a huge vacuum in our hearts which is extremely difficult to express. Without any doubts his departure is a major loss to his family and all of us. However, knowing him as long as I have known his character, I am confident that he wants us to look into the future instead of dwelling in the past. He wants us to remember and cherish the sweet memories and moments we have had together instead of recalling the difficult days he has had recently. 

 

I have to tell you my friend you have really done this time. Any excuse to gather your best friends into one room! But this time you have gone too far. Much too far. We will miss your kind words, great advice, and a level of energy that was highly contagious. We will miss all the great talk, the great conversation, the sharing of ideas. In fact now that I think about it, this is the only one-sided conversation I have ever had with you.

 

My friends, it is easy to feel sorry at a funeral: sorry for the departed one; sorry for the close grieving family; and sorry for oneself. I’ll tell you whom I feel sorry for today. I feel sorry for all the many people who did not have the rare and wonderful opportunity of meeting, of knowing, of working with my friend Farhang.

 

Per design of almighty Allah, in this life, nothing is forever and every beginning has an end. In Farhang’s case the end came when he was among caring and loving family members and friends. He left this life ready and in absolute peace of mind.

 

God bless his soul.
